Omics Data Analysis & Biomarker Identification

Our flagship GATomics (Graph Attention Network for Single-Cell Multi-Omics) platform revolutionizes single-cell data analysis by leveraging cutting-edge graph neural networks to uncover disease mechanisms and identify novel biomarkers with unprecedented accuracy. Ranked #1 in 57.9% of benchmark tests across 19 disease datasets.

Core Technology - GATomics

Graph Attention Networks Multi-Head Attention Cross-Stage Analysis scRNA-seq scATAC-seq snRNA-seq GPU Acceleration

Key Innovations

Multi-Stage Disease Modeling

Unlike traditional pairwise comparisons, GATomics simultaneously analyzes all disease stages (diagnosis, remission, relapse) in a unified framework, revealing stage-spanning regulatory genes

Graph-Based Cell Analysis

Models cells as nodes in a network with attention-weighted edges, capturing complex intercellular dynamics and microenvironmental influences

Dual-Penalty Mechanism

Intelligently filters housekeeping genes and non-coding RNAs to enhance disease-specific signal detection, particularly effective in solid tumors

Three-Pathway Gene Scoring

Identifies different gene categories: stage-specific markers, network organizers, and cross-stage regulators through attention-based, transformation-based, and structure-based analysis

Acute Myeloid Leukemia Multi-Stage Analysis

Novel Targets Found

GATomics analyzed 684,031 cells from 24 AML patients across diagnosis, remission, and relapse stages. While traditional tools missed them, GATomics identified critical chemoresistance genes:

  • RACK1: Promotes cancer stem cell self-renewal
  • NPM1: Maintains leukemic states through HOX regulation
  • HMGB1: Induces chemotherapy resistance via autophagy

These cross-stage regulators were ranked poorly (110th+) by conventional DEG analysis, demonstrating GATomics' unique capability.
